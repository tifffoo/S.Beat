Tinea pedis (Athlete's foot)

An itchy, fungal infection that is caused by sweaty, confined feet that are enclosed from air supply. This fungus can live in damp environments like the dorm room shower.

Symptoms include:

  • scaly red rash
  • blisters
  • ulcers
  • chronic dryness and scaling

Treatments include:

  • Over-the-counter topical antifungal medication (Desenex, Lamisil, Lotrinmin AF, Lotrimin Ultra, Tinactin)
  • Soak your feet in salt water or diluted vinegar to help dry up blisters.
  • Keep your feet dry and change socks regularly. When wearing shoes, make sure they are light and well ventilated.

Seeing a Doctor:

If over-the-counter medicine does not treat your infection, your doctor may offer topical or oral perscription antifungal medications.

Upper Respiratory Infection (Common Cold)

This is one of the most common viral infections of the nose and throat. If you are sneezing and have a runny nose, then the common cold is a strong possibility. Check the "influenza" or "allergies" pages to compare if you may relate more to those symptoms.

Symptoms include:

  • runny nose
  • cough
  • sore throat
  • congestion
  • watery eyes
  • sneezing
  • fatigue
  • fever
  • mild body aches

Treatments include:

  • Pain relievers(acetaminophen such as Tylenol)
  • Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin)
  • Cough syrup
  • Drink plenty of fluids like water and juice. Try to avoid the caffeine and alcohol (we know); they can dehydrate you. Warm soup is helpful, too!
  • Rest. If possible, stay home from school.
  • Adjust your room's temperature and humidity. Keep your room warm, but not overheated. Consider a humidifier if your room is dry.

You may have:

Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV)/AIDS

A sexually transmitted virus spread through certain body fluids (blood, semen, pre-seminal fluids, rectal fluids, vaginal fluids, and breast milk), HIV destroys many of the body's CD4 cells (aka T cells) if left untreated. These cells are essential in helping the body fight off infection and disease, and the damage seriously weakens the immune system.

Ways to prevent HIV:

Practicing safe sex and practices on a college campus is extremely important. Because there is no vaccine to prevent HIV and no cure for AIDS, take action before you or others get an infection.

  • Use a new condom every time you have sex. Stick to water-based lubricant (if you use it) rather than oil-based to prevent weakening/breaking of the condom. For oral sex, try using a dental dam.
  • Make sure to communicate with your sexual partner(s) if you or they have HIV.
  • If you use needles to inject drugs, make sure your needle is sterile and you keep it to yourself.

Symptoms include:

  • swollen lymph nodes
  • fever
  • diarrhea
  • unexplained fatigue
  • unusual infections
  • strange discharge
  • burning/painful urination
  • soaking night sweats

Treatments include:

HIV currently has no cure, but Antiretroviral therapy (ART) is reccommended for every who has HIV.

What is ART?

ART is a series of HIV medicines that work together to help and reduce the amount of HIV in the patient's blood. Talk to your physician about the regiment to learn more.
